1|61: Here is a simple and delicious recipe for baked salmon with a flavorful seasoning mix:  Ingredients: - 1 1/2 pounds or 4 (6-ounce) salmon fillets, wild caught if possible - Olive oil - 1 teaspoon kosher salt - ½ tablespoon brown sugar - 1 teaspoon smoked paprika - 1 teaspoon garlic powder - ½ teaspoon onion powder - ½ teaspoon dried thyme - ¼ teaspoon cumin  Instructions: 1. Preheat your oven to 400°F. Let the salmon come to room temperature before cooking. 2. (Optional) To reduce the white albumin on the salmon, brine it: mix 4 cups room temperature water with 3 tablespoons kosher salt until dissolved; place salmon in the brine for 15 minutes. 3. Line a baking sheet with aluminum foil and brush with olive oil. Pat the salmon fillets dry, place them skin side down on the foil, and brush with olive oil. 4. Sprinkle kosher salt evenly on the salmon pieces. 5. In a bowl, combine brown sugar, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, thyme, and cumin. Sprinkle this spice mixture evenly over the salmon. 6. Bake the salmon for 10 to 16 minutes depending on thickness, until it is just tender and pink in the center (internal temperature between 125 to 130°F). For a crispy top, when the salmon reaches 115°F, broil for about 2 minutes. 7. Remove from oven and serve.  This method ensures a tender salmon with a perfectly seasoned, slightly crispy top. It pairs well with a variety of sides like rice, quinoa, or roasted vegetables.

Place the salmon in this solution for 15 minutes while the oven preheats. 3. Place a large sheet of aluminum foil on a baking sheet and brush it with olive oil. 4. Pat each salmon fillet dry, place them skin side down on the foil, and brush the tops with olive oil. 5. Sprinkle the fillets evenly with kosher salt. 6. In a bowl, combine brown sugar, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, thyme, and cumin. Sprinkle this spice mixture evenly over the salmon. 7. Bake the salmon for 10 to 16 minutes depending on thickness, until just tender and pink in the center (aim for an internal temperature of 125 to 130°F). 8. For a crispy top, when the salmon reaches about 115°F, broil it for around 2 minutes near the end of cooking.  This results in tender, flavorful salmon with a nicely seasoned crust. Serve immediately and enjoy!
